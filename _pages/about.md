I am an undergraduate Computer Science researcher specializing in **Computer Vision**. My work centers on **medical imaging**, **Vision Language Models (VLMs)**, and perception systems for **autonomous vehicles**. I develop deep learning and transformer-based models that integrate multimodal data (imaging + clinical text + sensor streams) with a strong emphasis on explainable AI (XAI) to build clinically trustworthy and deployable intelligent systems.

Currently, I am advancing **Vision-Language Models** for medical understanding while leading projects in:
- Transformer-based radiomic token embedding for prostate cancer classification using mpMRI
- Time-aware multimodal models for antibiotic effectiveness and sepsis treatment outcome prediction from chest CT
- Histopathology classification (oral cancer) using YOLO26 vs CNN benchmarks
- Multimodal fusion for autonomous vehicle perception and clinical decision support

My long-term goal is to pursue a **Ph.D.** and develop robust, interpretable AI systems that bridge healthcare diagnostics and real-world autonomy.

**Research Interests**
- Medical Image Analysis (mpMRI, Histopathology, Chest CT)
- Vision Language Models & Multimodal Fusion
- Explainable AI (XAI) for Clinical Trust
- Computer Vision for Autonomous Vehicle Perception
